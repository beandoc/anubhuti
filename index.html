The Basics

Your Digestive System

Your digestive system (or "GI tract") is a group of organs that helps your body use food and drink. It turns food into fuel and removes waste as stool.

  • Food Pipe (Esophagus): The tube carrying food to your stomach.
  • Stomach: Uses acid to break food down.
  • Intestines: Where your body takes in nutrients.
  • Liver & Pancreas: Help make juices that digest food.

Understanding Indigestion

Decoding Dyspepsia

Indigestion (Dyspepsia) isn't just one thing. It's a set of symptoms like feeling full too quickly, upper belly pain, or a burning sensation.

The "75% Rule"

Did you know? For 3 out of 4 people, the stomach looks normal on tests, but doesn't *work* right. This is called Functional Indigestion.

Even if tests are normal, the pain is real. We focus on calming your stomach's sensitivity.

Red Flags: Act Now

See a doctor immediately for an Endoscopy if you notice any of these:

  • Weight Loss: Losing >5% of your weight without trying.
  • Difficulty Swallowing: Food feeling "stuck."
  • Constant Vomiting: Or seeing blood in vomit/stools.
  • Iron Deficiency: Low blood count (Anemia) you can't explain.

The Diagnostic Roadmap

Age Watch

If you are 60 or older, a camera test (Endoscopy) is usually the first step to be safe.

Germ Check

If you are under 60, we usually start with a simple breath or stool test for H. pylori germs.

Follow-up

If symptoms persist for 3-6 months, we look deeper with scans or biopsies.

Stomach Health & H. pylori

H. pylori is a common germ that affects the stomach. While often silent, it can cause pain, ulcers, or even more serious long-term issues if left untreated.

The Tests
  • Simple Breath Test
  • Simple Stool Test
  • (Blood tests are less reliable)
Warning Signs
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Black or tarry stools
Treatment Roadmap

Treatment usually involves a 2-week course of 2-4 medicines. It's vital to finish the full course, even if you feel better earlier. We re-test you 4 weeks later to ensure the germ is 100% gone.

Pro Tip: If a family member had stomach cancer, you should be tested for this germ.
Fatty Liver & Alcohol Care

Liver Health & Recovery

Your liver is your body's filter. Excessive alcohol or fat can damage it, leading to swelling or scarring (Cirrhosis).

Healing Steps
  • 150 mins brisk walking/week
  • 10-min "exercise snacks"
  • More fruits & vegetables
Strict No-Gos
  • Alcohol (No safe limit)
  • Tobacco / Smoking
  • High Fructose & Sugar
Why Quit Alcohol?

Stopping entirely is the only way to prevent severe liver damage and the risk of transplant.

IBD & Celiac Care

Digestive Harmony

Specialized care for Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD) and Celiac disease (Gluten sensitivity).

Understanding Celiac

Gluten (found in wheat, barley, rye) damages the small intestine. Symptoms range from diarrhea to anemia.

  • The Test: Simple blood test followed by a biopsy for confirmation.
  • Treatment: A strict, lifelong gluten-free diet is the only way to heal.
IBD & Biological Therapy

Managing Crohn's and Ulcerative Colitis through modern treatments focused on lasting remission and quality of life.

Warning Signs: If a gluten-free diet doesn't improve your symptoms, see your doctor immediately for further tests.

Diagnostic Tests

Upper Endoscopy (EGD)

An Upper Endoscopy is a safe test that lets your doctor see inside your food pipe, stomach, and the start of your intestine using a small camera.

How to Prepare
  • Fasting: No food for 6 to 8 hours. You can drink clear water until 2 hours before.
  • Medicines: Take your usual pills with a small sip of water, unless they are for diabetes or blood thinning. Ask us first!
  • Support: You will be sleepy after, so have a friend or family member ready to take you home.
Why is it done?

To find the cause of pain, weight loss, or trouble swallowing. Sometimes, we can also fix small problems during the test.

Safety First

This is a very safe test. Before we start, our team will check your heart and blood to make sure you can handle the sleep medicine safely.

Common Risks

Serious problems are very rare. The most common minor issues are a sore throat or feeling bloated for an hour.
